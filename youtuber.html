Do I even want to be a YouTuber?

Right now, I really don't know. If you ask me every other day, the answer might change.

Growing up, it's the second thing I ever wanted to be. First was an arcitect.

I was born in 2001 and started watching YouTube around 2007. My best friend Tyler Loughery put me onto it. He also showed me the depths of the internet.

A few years later, and he was posting his own videos on there. I forget if he had the Droid smartphone first or a camcorder but we made videos with whatever we could.

Every time I went over to his house, I wanted to make a video. We did skit type videos together.

Influenced by Smosh. They showed us what was possible on YouTube beyond home videos. I don't think I ever had the thought, "Are they making money off this?"

Maybe I assumed so since they were so popular but all I know is I wanted to make videos.

It was fun for Tyler and I. He did all of the editing too.

I just wanted to make videos with my best friend. Like Ian & Anthony were doing with Smosh.

It wasn't always fun. There were times we didn't want to make videos. There were times that Tyler specifically didn't want to.

It was probably because of how much work he had to do on the editing side. Maybe it was the social pressure that comes from posting yourself online.

Fast foward to today, and Tyler has overcome that social pressure in his own way. He is part of a band called ABBATIA with two other childhood friends of ours.
